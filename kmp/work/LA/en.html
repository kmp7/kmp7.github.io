Linguistic Analysis with LLM

Objective

Write a personal essay "My Winter Holidays with an LLM" and conduct a comprehensive computational linguistic analysis using a Large Language Model.

Essay length: ~2000 characters (with spaces), written in English.

Essay structure: 1st paragraph ‚Äî main holiday story; 2nd paragraph ‚Äî reflection on LLM use.

Step 1. Write the Essay

1

Write your essay independently (~2000 characters). First paragraph: describe your winter break. Second paragraph: reflect on how you used LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek, etc.) during the holidays or for your studies.

2

Prepare the text in English.

3

Save the text in a digital format (.txt or .docx) so you can copy it into an LLM chat.

Important: If any part of the essay was written or edited by an LLM, you must explicitly state which fragments and which tool were used.

LLM capabilities for linguistic analysis (English)

Modern LLMs can perform all 12 tasks, but accuracy varies by task type.

Reliability by task type

High reliability

Semantic & stylistic levels ‚Äî strong suit of all leading LLMs.

TaskNotes
Topic identificationExcellent at thematic grouping.
Sentiment & styleConfidently distinguishes register, polarity; provides justifications.
ParaphrasingCore capability; formal/colloquial variants.
Named Entity RecognitionReliably extracts names, places, dates, tools.
Metacognition (hypotheses)Creative, insightful generation.
Coherence suggestionsQuality advice on text flow.

Medium reliability

Morphology & syntax ‚Äî generally correct, but ambiguous cases cause errors.

TaskTypical issues
POS taggingErrors with homonyms: "record" (noun/verb), "lead", "wind".
LemmatizationUsually correct, but may struggle with irregular forms.
Dependency parsingApproximate, not a formal parser ‚Äî possible inaccuracies in complex sentences.
Error detectionGood at gross spelling errors; subtle agreement issues may be missed or produce false positives.

Low reliability

Exact counts ‚Äî fundamental architectural limitation.

TaskWhy LLMs fail
Character countProcess text as tokens, not characters; one token = 1‚Äì4+ characters.
Word countApproximate; errors 5‚Äì15% common.
Average word lengthResult of inaccurate counts.
Sentence countProblems with abbreviations (e.g., "Dr.", "e.g."), quotes, ellipses.
